Threats – the short story

  • Wireless introduces new problems, but also reintroduces old problems

  • Attacks:

    • Eavesdropping (it’s easier to eavesdrop wireless communication). Prevented by encryption.
    • Inject packets. Prevented by performing source authentication.
    • Modify packets. Avoided by doing message integrity.
    • Replay packets. Prevented using timestamps and sequence numbers for packets.
    • Perform denial of service by intentionally dropping packets or misrouting them.
