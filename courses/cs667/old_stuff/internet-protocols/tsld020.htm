Why have different routing protocols?

  • OSPF sounds like it is so automatic and fast, why not use it everywhere?

    • Well, there are a couple of problems.
      • First, once a route is in the OSPF network, it cannot be removed. This creates problems with certain routing policies.
      • Second, OSPF gives you very little control over what routes you announce. Once a route is in the OSPF network, it must go to every router in said network.
      • This is why there are simpler protocols like BGP4.
