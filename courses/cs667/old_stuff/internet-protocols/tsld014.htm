Inter-AS Routing (Aggregation)

  • Does this cause any problems?

    • What it means is that routers that are not in the AS that originated the announcement have very limited knowledge of exactly where the destination is.
    • Because of this limited knowledge, networks do what is known as closest exit routing.
      • This operates under the assumption that the destination network has more specific knowledge of the destination than I do, so I will get said traffic out of my network as quickly as possible.
