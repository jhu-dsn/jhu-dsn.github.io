System streams are meta-streams that include one or more video and audio streams as well as time-stamp information necessary to synchronize the audio and video tracks.

Video Streams

MPEG is a Complex encoding!

Actually uses 4 types of compression:

  • Subsampling the source
  • Discrete Cosine Transformation
  • Huffman encoding and RLE
  • Motion compensation

Inter-frame Motion Compression

The P and B frames can be much smaller then the I frames because of the small displacements of identifiable patterns in the picture. B frames are not used as a base for the prediction of other B or P frames, only I and P frames are used for this.

MPEG-1 allows accuracy to 1/2 or 1 Pel, and searches upto +-128 Pels for the best match.

Audio Streams

  • Encoded at 8Kbs-256Kbs
  • Usually sampled at 44kHZ
