Present Situation
People, businesses, and managers love the "Internet". It is not the Internet as a whole that they like, but TCP/IP traffic on ports 80 and 25 (HTTP and SMTP (typical email), respectively) that drive commerce. To businesses that is the Internet. Now we have immense amounts of HTTP traffic clogging the "pipes" of the current Internet plumbing system. Much focus, now, is going into reducing the amount of unnecessary traffic. Most approaches to this problem attempt some form of replicated information at key interconnects in the topology of the network.

These solutions will help make things more effecient even if in a few years, bandwidth will increase by a factor of 10 or 100. People should never need a reason to implement things in the most effecient manner, becuase the demand placed on the system will almost always match the physical resources available.

Future Directions
As demand for "neat" things increases on the Internet, solutions to provide these things seemlessly to the user will evolve. Many things start out as "neat" and turn into a powerful and useful tools for everyday work. No matter how large the Internet gets and no matter how "big the pipes get", demand will push the system to the limit. Servers will become faster to serve even more information and clients will become faster and greedier, wanting to pull more information.

The speed of the personal computer will evolve as it has since its birth and the need for distributed computing will be both inside and outside of the box.
