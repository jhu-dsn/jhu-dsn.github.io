HTTP/1.1 Check out the RFC
  • Multiple transactions per connection.
  • Allowable transactions are more robust, additions include:
  • OPTIONS
  • PUT
  • DELETE
  • TRACE
  • "Document expiration" concept implemented.
  • More complex conditional retrieval
  • Virtual hosting was made possible

    • Motiviations: As HTTP became one of the major means of transactions on the Internet, optimization and functionality were found to be limited. HTTP/1.0 was revised for use and optimization, leading to better bandwidth utilization, less underlying protocol overhead, and less IP address waste.
